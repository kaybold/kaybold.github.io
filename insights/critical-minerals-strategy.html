Critical minerals strategy: beyond extraction

Owning the resource is not strategy. The question is who captures value and how institutional capacity evolves.

Countries rich in critical minerals face a familiar pattern: extraction accelerates, but value capture remains limited. The transition economy creates urgency, but urgency alone does not produce better outcomes.

Strategy requires clarity on three questions: What share of the value chain can realistically be captured in the medium term? What institutional capabilities must be built to support that position? And what governance structures prevent value leakage?

Without answers to these questions, resource wealth becomes another iteration of the same extractive cycle. The opportunity of the energy transition is not automatic. It must be designed.
