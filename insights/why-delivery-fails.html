Why delivery fails before implementation begins

March 2026

Reform failure is rarely technical. It begins with misaligned incentives, unclear authority, and unrealistic sequencing.

Most delivery challenges surface long before implementation starts. The root causes are predictable: competing priorities among stakeholders, ambiguous decision rights, insufficient political cover, or timelines that ignore institutional capacity.

Effective delivery requires more than good plans. It requires clarity about who decides, who benefits, who bears costs, and what happens when priorities conflict. Without this groundwork, even well-resourced initiatives stall.

The question is not whether obstacles will arise. The question is whether leaders have the structures, authority, and accountability routines to navigate them.
